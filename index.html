About The Program

The Joan Miller Womack Weweso School Development Program is dedicated to improving the educational opportunities for children in Kumasi, Ghana. Founded by Joan Miller Womack, the program seeks to address the significant resource gaps faced by local schools, ensuring that every child has access to quality education.

Our Mission

Our mission is to provide essential resources and support to the Weweso Junior High School and Asuokoo D/A Basic School. We aim to create a conducive learning environment where students can thrive academically and personally. By offering resources such as computers, laptops, desks, books, and other school supplies, we strive to enhance the educational experience and outcomes for these children.

About The Schools

Weweso Junior High School

This government school lacks adequate funding for necessary teaching and learning materials to improve the quality of education.

  • Government school

  • Part of the free compulsory universal basic education

  • Lacks adequate funding

  • Insufficient teaching and learning materials

Asuokoo D/A Basic School

Established by the community to provide formal education and reduce illiteracy. Now the school has over 235 students with 11 classrooms.

  • Community-established school

  • Initial enrollment: 20 boys and 12 girls

  • Current enrollment: 235+ students

  • 11 classrooms, 8 primary teachers, 8 junior high school teachers

Services

What Can We Do For Our Schools?

Here are some of the key services and support we provide to enhance the educational experience for students at Weweso Junior High School and Asuokoo D/A Basic School.

Provision of Learning Materials

We supply essential learning materials including textbooks, exercise books, and stationery to ensure students have the resources they need to succeed.

Teacher Training Workshops

We organize workshops to enhance the teaching skills of educators, focusing on modern teaching methodologies and effective classroom management techniques.

Infrastructure Development

Our program supports the construction and renovation of classrooms and other school facilities to provide a conducive learning environment.

Digital Literacy Programs

We introduce students to basic computer skills and internet usage, preparing them for the digital world and enhancing their learning experience.

Health and Wellness Initiatives

We promote the physical and mental well-being of students by organizing health screenings, providing hygiene products, and conducting wellness workshops.
